Prefix & Suffix Reference Guide

Understanding prefixes and suffixes helps you decode unfamiliar words and spell them correctly.

I Before E Rule

I before E, except after C, or when sounded like A as in "neighbor" and "weigh".

Examples: receive, conceive, perceive (after C) | believe, achieve, field (i before e)

Exceptions: weird, seize, leisure, neither, protein, caffeine

Doubling Consonants

When adding a suffix starting with a vowel to a word ending in a single consonant preceded by a single vowel, double the consonant if the word is one syllable or the stress is on the last syllable.

Examples: run → running, begin → beginning, stop → stopped

Do NOT double: visit → visiting (stress on first syllable)

Silent E Rule

Drop the silent E when adding a suffix that begins with a vowel. Keep it when the suffix begins with a consonant.

Drop: make → making, drive → driving

Keep: care → careful, hope → hopeful

Exceptions: noticeable, courageous (keep E after C or G before -able/-ous)

Y to I Rule

When a word ends in a consonant + Y, change Y to I before adding a suffix (unless the suffix starts with I).

Examples: happy → happiness, carry → carried, beauty → beautiful

Keep Y before -ing: carry → carrying, study → studying

-CEDE, -CEED, -SEDE

Only one word ends in -sede: supersede. Only three words end in -ceed: exceed, proceed, succeed. All others end in -cede.

Examples: precede, concede, recede, intercede, accede

-ABLE vs -IBLE

Use -able if you can add it to a complete word. Use -ible if removing the suffix doesn't leave a complete word.

-able: comfortable, reasonable, dependable

-ible: terrible, visible, possible

Foreign Word Markings

UIL Spelling includes words from foreign languages. Accent marks must be used correctly:

Acute accent (é): pronounced as long "a" - dégagé, émigré

Grave accent (è): "eh" sound - crème

Circumflex (ê): "eh" or "oh" - crêpe

Cedilla (ç): "s" sound - façade, garçon

Umlaut (ü): modified vowel - naïve

Note: Improper use of accent marks or omitting them is considered a spelling error in UIL competition.
